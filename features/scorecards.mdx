Scorecards give your hiring team a shared, structured way to evaluate every candidate against the same criteria. Instead of gathering informal impressions after the fact, you define what "good" looks like before interviews begin — and every interviewer rates against that same bar.

## Create a scorecard for a role

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Scorecard tab">
    Navigate to the job and click the **Scorecard** tab.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add your criteria">
    Click **Add criterion** and give it a name. Common examples include:

    - Technical skills
    - Communication
    - Problem-solving
    - Culture add
    - Role-specific competencies

    Add as many criteria as the role requires.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set the rating scale">
    Choose the numeric scale interviewers will use — for example, 1–5. The same scale applies to every criterion on the scorecard.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add notes prompts (optional)">
    For any criterion, you can add a prompt to guide interviewers — for example, "Describe a specific example the candidate gave." Prompts are visible to the interviewer when they fill in their scorecard.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save the scorecard">
    Click **Save**. This scorecard now applies to all interviews for this role. Every interviewer who submits feedback on a candidate for this job uses this scorecard.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  Define your scorecard criteria before interviews begin, not after. It ensures every interviewer evaluates candidates against the same standard — and makes your debrief conversations much faster.
</Tip>

## Submit a scorecard as an interviewer

After you finish an interview, record your assessment directly in Join.

1. Open the candidate profile and click the **Scorecards** tab.
2. Rate each criterion on the defined scale.
3. Add notes for any criterion where you want to explain your rating or flag a specific example.
4. Click **Submit scorecard**.

Your ratings are added to the panel aggregate immediately. You can edit your scorecard until the hiring decision is made.

## Review the full panel's scores

Once interviewers start submitting scorecards, the candidate profile gives you a complete picture of the panel's assessment.

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Side-by-side comparison" icon="table-columns">
    See every interviewer's ratings for each criterion next to each other. Spot immediately where the panel agrees and where one interviewer is an outlier.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Aggregate scores" icon="chart-bar">
    Join calculates the panel average per criterion and overall. Use the aggregate to anchor your debrief discussion rather than starting from scratch.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

Use the side-by-side view to run your debrief. Where scores diverge, let the data prompt the conversation — the goal is to surface evidence, not just opinions.

<Note>
  Scorecards are visible to everyone with access to the candidate profile. The notes interviewers add alongside their ratings are internal — candidates never see them.
</Note>
