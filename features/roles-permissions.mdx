Join uses a two-tier role model to give you precise control over who sees which jobs and what they can do with them. A company-level role sets the broadest boundary of access for each person on your account; a per-job role then defines exactly what that person can do on each individual role they're involved in. Together, the two tiers let you keep sensitive hiring information private while giving every interviewer and hiring manager exactly the access they need.

## Company-level roles

Your company-level role determines which jobs you can see at all. Users without System admin access only ever see the jobs they've been explicitly added to.

| Role | Access | Typical use |
|---|---|---|
| **System admin** | Full access to all jobs, candidates, and account settings | HR lead, recruiting ops |
| **Standard** | Only jobs they're explicitly added to | Recruiter, coordinator |
| **Limited** | Most restricted view; no access to jobs unless added | External agency, part-time reviewer |

## Per-job roles

Once someone is added to a job, their per-job role controls what they can actually do on that role.

| Role | What they can do |
|---|---|
| **Job admin** | Create, edit, publish, and archive the job; manage the hiring team on the job |
| **Hiring manager** | Review candidates and make hiring decisions |
| **Reviewer** | Interview candidates and submit scorecards |

## How the two tiers combine

The two tiers work together — they don't override each other.

- A person's **company-level role** sets the ceiling of what they can ever see across the account.
- Their **per-job role** controls what they can do on each specific job they're added to.
- The same person can hold different per-job roles on different jobs. For example, a Standard user might be the **Job admin** on an open engineering role they're leading, but only a **Reviewer** on a sales role where they're conducting one interview.

This means you can give a recruiter ownership of their own roles without granting them visibility into every other team's hiring.

## Invite external collaborators

You can bring in people from outside your company — agency recruiters, freelance sourcers, or interview panellists — and scope their access to a single job.

- Invite them by email and assign a per-job role on only the relevant jobs.
- External collaborators never see your other jobs, candidates, or account settings.
- Removing them from a job immediately revokes that access — no delays, no lingering permissions.
