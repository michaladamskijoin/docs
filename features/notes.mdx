Notes give your hiring team a permanent, structured place to record observations, flag concerns, and share context — all attached directly to the candidate profile. Unlike a Slack thread or email chain, notes stay with the candidate forever and are always exactly one click away.

## Add a note

Open the candidate profile, click the **Notes** tab, and start writing in the rich text editor. You can format your note with headings, bullet lists, and bold text, then attach supporting files before you save.

**Supported attachment types:**
- PDF documents (interview guides, CVs, assessments)
- Audio files (recorded interviews or voice memos)
- Screenshots and images

Click **Save note** when you're done. The note appears instantly for everyone who has the right visibility level.

## Note visibility

Control exactly who can read each note with two visibility settings.

| Visibility | Who sees it |
|---|---|
| **Private** | You and any teammates you @-mention in the note |
| **Team** | Everyone with access to the candidate profile |

The default visibility is **Team**. Toggle it to **Private** before saving if the note contains sensitive observations you want to limit — for example, early-stage salary expectations or a concern you want to raise with the hiring manager only.

## @-mention a teammate

Pull a specific colleague into a note without broadcasting it to the whole team.

- Type `@` followed by their name anywhere in the note body.
- They receive an **in-app notification** and an **email** that links directly to the note.
- Mentioning someone in a Private note grants them visibility on that note only — they won't see your other private notes on the same candidate.

## Emoji reactions

React to a teammate's note with an emoji to acknowledge it quickly without writing a full reply. A 👍 after a hiring manager's feedback signals you've seen it; a ⚠️ flags something worth revisiting — all without cluttering the notes thread.

<Info>
  Notes are strictly internal. Candidates never see them, even if you share a candidate profile externally for a reference check or panel review.
</Info>

<Note>
  Notes and scorecards serve different purposes: notes are free-form context and gut reactions; scorecards are structured ratings against pre-defined criteria. Use both — notes for the colour, scorecards for the record.
</Note>
