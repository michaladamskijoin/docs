Join's hiring pipeline gives every recruiter and hiring manager a single, live view of where every candidate stands. Whether you prefer a visual kanban board or a spreadsheet-style list, you and your team see the same data — updated in real time as anyone on the role makes a move.

## Navigate the pipeline

The pipeline opens as a **kanban board** by default. Each column is a stage in your hiring process, and each card is a candidate. As candidates progress, they move left to right across the board.

- **Board view** — best for day-to-day recruiting. See candidate context at a glance and drag cards to advance them.
- **List view** — best for high-volume rounds. See all candidates in a sortable table and take bulk actions fast.

Toggle between views using the **Board / List** switcher at the top of the pipeline. Both views stay in sync: any change one team member makes appears for everyone else on the role in real time.

## Move and manage candidates

**Drag to advance a stage**

Drag a candidate card from one column to the next to move them forward in your process. The move is logged automatically with a timestamp and the user who made it.

**Drop to reject**

Drag a card to the **Rejected** column to reject a candidate. Join prompts you to select a structured reason before the move completes:

- Skills mismatch
- Compensation expectations
- Timing
- Other

Choosing a reason keeps your rejection data consistent and your audit trail clean.

**Bulk-move and bulk-reject**

Select up to 20 candidate cards at once using the checkboxes in list view, then choose **Move to stage** or **Reject** from the bulk-action toolbar. Bulk rejects prompt for a templated reason and send the appropriate rejection communications automatically.

<Note>
  Every bulk action is audit-logged and reversible. You can restore a rejected candidate in one click from their profile — nothing is permanently deleted.
</Note>

You can define the stages for a role in two ways:

  <Card title="Let Join AI propose stages" icon="sparkles" href="/features/pipeline#set-up-pipeline-stages">

Either way, stages are fully customisable per role — add, rename, reorder, or remove stages before you publish the job, or at any point while it's live.

## Spot bottlenecks

The pipeline surfaces two types of signal to help you act before a slow stage costs you a candidate.

**Time-in-stage indicators**

Each candidate card shows how long the candidate has been sitting in the current stage. Cards that exceed your configured threshold turn amber, then red, so you can prioritise follow-up at a glance.

**Stage-conversion metrics**

Open the **Metrics** panel on any pipeline to see conversion rates between every consecutive stage — apply-to-screen, screen-to-interview, interview-to-offer, and offer acceptance. A sharp drop between two stages points to exactly where your funnel is leaking.

<Tip>
  Use the list view for high-volume screening rounds — it's faster for bulk actions than the kanban board.
</Tip>
