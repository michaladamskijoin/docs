Multiposting lets you write your job ad once in Join and distribute it across more than 250 job boards simultaneously — organic boards included by default, paid boards added with a click. Every application routes straight into your Join pipeline with its source board tagged, so you always know which channels deliver results.

## How multiposting works

<Steps>
  <Step title="Create or import your job ad in Join">
    Write your job description in Join's editor or generate it with the [AI Job Description Generator](/features/job-description-generator). You only ever maintain one version of the ad.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select your boards">
    Organic boards are selected by default. Browse the paid board catalogue and add regional or niche boards as needed — filter by country, industry, or audience.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click Publish">
    Organic boards go live within minutes. Paid boards go live the same day, after each board's standard moderation step.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Edit once, update everywhere">
    Changed the salary band or closing date? Edit the ad in Join and every connected board updates automatically — no logging in to individual portals.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Quick Apply

Quick Apply lets candidates submit their application directly on the job board without leaving that site or filling in a second form. Join receives the application instantly and tags it with the source board — the candidate experience is frictionless, and your pipeline stays complete.

Quick Apply is supported on seven boards:

## Track which boards perform

Every application that comes in through a multiposted job is automatically tagged with its source board. Open the job's analytics panel to see:

- **Apply-to-interview rate** per board — which boards send candidates worth interviewing
- **Apply-to-hire rate** per board — which boards deliver hires, not just volume

Use this data to shift budget toward the boards that work for your roles and pull spend from the ones that don't. Analytics update in real time as candidates move through your pipeline.
