Join's AI Job Description Generator replaces the blank-page problem with a guided wizard that asks the right questions, drafts a complete structured job ad, and then checks it for biased language and missing search keywords — all before you publish. Every block is editable, so you stay in control of the final copy.

## How the wizard works

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open a new job and click Generate with AI">
    Start a new job from your dashboard and select **Generate with AI** instead of writing from scratch. The wizard opens in a side panel alongside the editor.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Complete the wizard">
    Answer a short set of questions about the role:

    - **Role title and location** — used for SEO targeting and salary benchmarking
    - **Salary band** — Join suggests a range based on live market data for the role and location; accept it, adjust it, or leave it blank
    - **Benefits** — pulled from your workspace profile automatically; add or remove as needed
    - **Key requirements** — list the skills, experience level, and any must-have qualifications
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review the generated ad">
    Join drafts a full, structured job ad with four sections:

    1. **About the company** — drawn from your workspace profile
    2. **Responsibilities** — role-specific bullet points based on the title and requirements you provided
    3. **Requirements** — formatted candidate criteria, split into must-have and nice-to-have
    4. **Benefits** — your benefits list, formatted consistently
  </Step>
  <Step title="Edit any block">
    Click into any section to rewrite, expand, or trim the copy. Nothing is locked in — the AI output is a starting point, not a final draft you have to accept wholesale.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set tone and language, then publish">
    Choose a tone preset to match your employer brand, or translate the ad to another language before sending it to your selected job boards.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Inclusive language and SEO

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Bias detection">
    Join's bias checker scans the draft for language patterns that can discourage qualified candidates from applying and flags them inline with suggested alternatives.

    The checker identifies:

    - **Gendered language** — words and phrases that skew toward a particular gender (e.g. "rockstar", "nurturing")
    - **Age-coded language** — terms that imply a preferred age range (e.g. "recent graduate", "seasoned professional")
    - **Ableist language** — requirements framed around ability rather than the actual task (e.g. "must be able to stand for 8 hours" when the role is desk-based)

    Each flag shows the original phrase, explains why it may deter applicants, and offers one or more inclusive alternatives. Accept or dismiss each suggestion individually.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="SEO keyword coach">
    The SEO coach suggests the search terms candidates actually type into Indeed, LinkedIn, and Google for Jobs when looking for a role like yours. These are not generic keywords — they are derived from real search volume data for the role title and location you entered.

    Suggestions appear as chips alongside the relevant section. Click a chip to insert the keyword into the copy, or drag it to the position that reads most naturally. The coach also highlights keyword gaps if your draft is missing high-volume terms your competitors are likely using.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

**Supported languages:**

- 🇫🇷 French
- 🇩🇪 German
- 🇪🇸 Spanish
- 🇮🇹 Italian
- 🇳🇱 Dutch

You can publish different language versions to different boards from the same job record — no need to create separate jobs for each market. Analytics from all language versions roll up into a single job report.
