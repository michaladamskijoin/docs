## At a glance

Eligible audience

Young people 16–25, job-seekers 26 and over, recipients of social minimums

Duration

6 to 12 months generally. Up to 24 months for some audiences and 36 months for priority audiences.

Legal form

CDD or CDI (with a professionalization phase at start)

Training time

15 to 25 % of total contract duration (minimum 150 hours)

Minimum compensation

Between 55 % and 100 % of SMIC depending on age and qualification level. See the in-contract table.

Available funding

OPCO covers training costs. Hiring aid depending on profile. Reduced social-security contributions.

## Adapting the template

- **Priority audiences.** Duration extendable up to 36 months for recipients of social minimums and specific situations (disability, exit from an integration scheme).
- **In-house training.** Allowed if the company has an identified training department, with pedagogical resources distinct from the production unit. Justify it in the contract.
- **CDI with a professionalization phase.** Lets you hire directly on a CDI while still benefiting from OPCO funding for the initial qualification phase.
- **Hiring aid.** Verify eligibility with the OPCO or France Travail before signing. Amount and conditions evolve.

## What employers miss

- ### OPCO funding is conditional
 
 The OPCO (skills operator) covers all or part of the training costs. The file must be submitted before the contract starts. A file submitted after the start exposes the employer to a refusal of funding.
 
- ### Cerfa 12434\*04 is mandatory
 
 The contract must be filed on Cerfa form 12434\*04 and submitted to the OPCO within 5 days of the start. The OPCO reviews the file and forwards it to the Dreets. Overall delay is about 20 days for validation.
 
- ### Tutor required
 
 A tutor is designated among the company's employees, with at least 2 years of experience related to the target qualification. The tutor cannot supervise more than 3 work-study trainees simultaneously (2 if the tutor is the employer themselves).
 
- ### Statutory pay scale
 
 Under 26: 55 % to 80 % of SMIC depending on age and qualification level before the contract. 26 and over: minimum 100 % of SMIC or 85 % of the collective-agreement minimum if more favorable. The collective agreement may require better.
 
- ### Qualification must be RNCP-listed
 
 The contract must target a qualification listed in the RNCP, a branch CQP, or a qualification recognized in the applicable collective agreement. Training represents 15 to 25 % of total duration (minimum 150 hours). Outside a recognized qualification or outside that hour range: clawback by the OPCO or the Dreets.
 

## Frequently asked questions

- Who is eligible for a professionalization contract?
 
 The contrat de professionnalisation (professionalization contract, a work-study arrangement) is aimed at young people aged 16 to 25 completing their initial training, job-seekers aged 26 and over, and recipients of certain social minimum benefits. It can be signed as a CDD or as a CDI that begins with a professionalization phase.
 
- How long does a professionalization contract last, and how much of it is training?
 
 Most contracts run from 6 to 12 months, extending to 24 months for some groups and up to 36 for priority audiences such as benefit recipients. Training has to make up 15% to 25% of the total duration, with a floor of 150 hours, and it must lead to a qualification listed in the RNCP, a branch CQP, or a qualification recognised by the collective agreement.
 
- How much is a work-study trainee paid?
 
 Pay follows a statutory scale tied to age and prior qualification: from 55% of the SMIC for the under-21s without a qualification, up to 100% of the SMIC (or 85% of the collective-agreement minimum, if that is more favourable) for trainees aged 26 and over. The collective agreement can set higher rates, so check it before fixing the figure.
 
- What does the OPCO fund, and what has to happen for the funding to come through?
 
 The OPCO (skills operator) covers all or part of the training and tutoring costs, but the funding is conditional. The file has to be submitted on Cerfa form 12434\*04 within five days of the start, after which the OPCO forwards it to the Dreets, with validation taking around 20 days. Submit the file once the contract has already begun and you risk an outright refusal, plus a clawback if the qualification or training hours fall outside what the scheme recognises.
 
- Who can act as the tutor?
 
 The tutor is chosen from among the company's employees and needs at least two years of experience related to the target qualification. One tutor can support up to three work-study trainees at once, or two if the employer takes on the role personally.
