## Adapting the template

- **Minor apprentice.** Working time limited to 8 h/day and 35 h/week. Hazardous tasks forbidden (derogations possible by decree). Legal guardian present at signature.
- **Apprentice with a disability.** Duration extendable to 4 years. Adjustments possible (training time, working conditions). Specific aids from AGEFIPH.
- **Preparing the same diploma at a higher level.** Possibility to extend or sign a new contract with the same employer, without additional probation if the training is continuous.
- **Post-apprenticeship hire.** If the apprentice is then hired on a CDI in the same role, no probation period. Seniority starts at the initial apprenticeship hire date.

## What employers miss

- ### CFA registration is mandatory upfront
 
 The apprentice must be enrolled in a Centre de Formation d'Apprentis (CFA) or apprenticeship section. Enrolment precedes the contract signature. The CFA delivers theoretical training and signs a tripartite agreement with the employer and apprentice.
 
- ### Qualified apprenticeship master
 
 An apprenticeship master is appointed within the company. Conditions: a diploma in the same field and 1 year of practice, or 2 years of branch experience without a diploma. The master cannot supervise more than 2 apprentices simultaneously (3 in case of repeated year).
 
- ### No precarity premium
 
 At the end of the apprenticeship contract, the 10 % precarity premium does not apply. The contract derogates from the standard CDD regime. The apprentice does receive their final salary according to the applicable scale until the last day.
 
- ### Free rupture during the first 45 days
 
 During the 45 days in the company (apprenticeship probation), either party can terminate freely, without motive or compensation. Beyond that, termination is strictly framed: gross misconduct, force majeure, medical unfitness, express written agreement of both parties.
 
- ### Cerfa FA13 and OPCO submission within 5 days
 
 The contract must be filed on Cerfa form FA13 (or its dematerialized equivalent via the OPCO) and submitted to the OPCO within 5 days of the start. Without OPCO submission: no funding of training costs and no eligibility for hiring aid.
 

## Frequently asked questions

- Who can be hired on an apprenticeship contract in France?
 
 Apprenticeships are open to people aged 16 to 29. Several groups sit outside that range: there is no upper age limit for people with a disability, high-level athletes, or those creating or taking over a business. The contract runs from 6 months to 3 years, up to 4 years for an apprentice with a disability, and takes the form of a specific CDD or a CDI that opens with an apprenticeship phase.
 
- How is an apprentice paid?
 
 Apprentice pay follows a statutory scale set as a percentage of the SMIC, rising with age and year of study: from 27% for the youngest first-years up to 100% of the SMIC (or the collective-agreement minimum, if higher) for apprentices aged 26 and over. Pay increases automatically on the contract's anniversary date or when the apprentice moves into a new age bracket.
 
- Does an apprentice have to be enrolled in a CFA?
 
 Yes, and enrolment in a Centre de Formation d'Apprentis (CFA, apprentice training centre) is a precondition, not a formality that follows the hire. The apprentice must be registered before the contract is signed. The CFA delivers the classroom training and signs a three-way agreement with the employer and apprentice.
 
- Who can act as the apprenticeship master, and how many apprentices can they supervise?
 
 The maître d'apprentissage (apprenticeship master) needs a diploma in the same field plus one year of practice, or two years of relevant branch experience without a diploma. A single master can supervise at most two apprentices at a time, or three if one of them is repeating a year.
 
- Is a precarity premium due at the end of an apprenticeship?
 
 Unlike an ordinary CDD, an apprenticeship contract ends without the 10% precarity premium, because the scheme derogates from the standard fixed-term rules. The apprentice is still paid their full salary under the applicable scale right up to the last day. If you then hire them on a CDI in the same role, no fresh probation applies and their apprenticeship seniority carries over.
 
- What financial support is available for hiring an apprentice in 2026?
 
 For 2026, employers can claim hiring aid of €6,000 for the first year, subject to annual renewal of the scheme. The funding depends on filing the contract on Cerfa form FA13 (or its digital equivalent) and submitting it to the OPCO (skills operator) within five days of the start; miss that step and you lose both the training funding and the aid. Confirm the current amount and conditions with your OPCO before signing.
