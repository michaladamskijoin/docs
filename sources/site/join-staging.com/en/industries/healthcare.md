Is candidate data stored in the EU?

Yes. Hosting, processing, and backups all sit in EU regions. The sub-processor list is published and SCCs are covered for any non-EU integration.

How does Join handle medical credentials?

Knockout questions confirm registration number, language of care, right-to-practise, and shift pattern before a CV is reviewed. Specialty tagging keeps credential evidence on the candidate profile through the whole pipeline.

Are knockout-screening decisions auditable under the EU AI Act?

Yes. Every Knockout decision is logged with the candidate's answer and the rule that matched, and a recruiter can reverse a wrong call. That's useful for GDPR audits and the EU AI Act's human-oversight requirement on candidate-decision systems.

Can I run separate pipelines per clinic or specialty?

Yes. Each clinic, dental practice, or specialty can have its own pipeline. Roles and permissions match the org chart, so finance, the practice manager, and senior clinicians each see only what they need.

Does Join integrate with HR or rota systems?

Native Zapier and Make apps cover most HRIS and rota tools. A public REST API and webhooks handle direct integrations, so hired-candidate data lands in payroll or scheduling without manual rekeying.

Can we restrict who sees which candidates?

Yes, at the job level: senior clinicians and the practice manager see the roles they're assigned to, and others don't see roles they're not on. There's no per-field hiding within a profile today, so anyone with access to a candidate sees the full profile, salary included.
