# Source: https://join-staging.com/en/glossary

## A

- [**Active candidate**\\ \\ An active candidate is someone currently looking for a job — sending applications, updating their LinkedIn, reading job boards. The opposite of a passive candidate.\\ \\ Sourcing & channels](https://join-staging.com/en/glossary/active-candidate)
- [**AGG (Germany)**\\ \\ AGG (Allgemeines Gleichbehandlungsgesetz) is Germany's anti-discrimination law. It governs what you can and cannot ask, mention, or weigh in a hiring decision in Germany.\\ \\ Compliance & legal](https://join-staging.com/en/glossary/agg)
- [**AI bias in hiring**\\ \\ AI bias in hiring is the tendency of machine-learning models to reproduce — and sometimes amplify — patterns of discrimination from the data they were trained on. The reason 'AI fairness in hiring' is a research field, not a checkbox.\\ \\ AI & automation](https://join-staging.com/en/glossary/ai-bias-in-hiring)
- [**AI candidate matching**\\ \\ AI candidate matching scores how well a candidate fits a specific role by comparing CV signals (skills, experience, seniority) to the role's requirements. The mechanism behind 'AI screening' and 'AI sourcing.'\\ \\ AI & automation](https://join-staging.com/en/glossary/ai-candidate-matching)
- [**AI in recruiting**\\ \\ AI in recruiting is the use of machine-learning models in hiring — for screening, sourcing, scheduling, candidate matching, and rejection. Most ATS platforms now ship some form of it; the question is which parts are useful.\\ \\ AI & automation](https://join-staging.com/en/glossary/ai-in-recruiting)
- [**AI screening**\\ \\ AI screening uses machine-learning models to score applications against role criteria — surfacing strong matches and demoting weak ones for human review. Faster than manual CV review, but only as good as the criteria it scores against.\\ \\ AI & automation](https://join-staging.com/en/glossary/ai-screening)
- [**AI sourcing**\\ \\ AI sourcing uses machine-learning models to find candidates outside your application pipeline — searching LinkedIn, GitHub, public profiles, and your existing talent pool to surface people who match a role. Outbound, not inbound.\\ \\ AI & automation](https://join-staging.com/en/glossary/ai-sourcing)
- [**Applicant tracking system**\\ \\ An applicant tracking system is software that collects job applications, keeps them in one place, and helps a hiring team move candidates through stages.\\ \\ ATS & tools](https://join-staging.com/en/glossary/applicant-tracking-system)
- [**Applicants per hire**\\ \\ Applicants per hire is the ratio of total applications received to hires made for a role. Reveals how selective your process is and how well your top-of-funnel is performing.\\ \\ Metrics](https://join-staging.com/en/glossary/applicants-per-hire)
- [**Application completion rate**\\ \\ Application completion rate is the percentage of candidates who start the application form and finish it. The clearest top-of-funnel leak metric; sub-50% rates usually point at form length or unnecessary fields.\\ \\ Metrics](https://join-staging.com/en/glossary/application-completion-rate)
- [**Application form**\\ \\ An application form is the structured form a candidate fills out to apply for a role — fields like name, contact, CV upload, and any role-specific questions. The single biggest leak point in most SMB hiring funnels.\\ \\ Process & stages](https://join-staging.com/en/glossary/application-form)
- [**Apprenticeship**\\ \\ An apprenticeship is a multi-year hybrid of formal training and paid work — common in Germany (Ausbildung) and increasingly elsewhere in Europe. Builds a candidate from school-leaver to qualified worker, paid throughout.\\ \\ Sourcing & channels](https://join-staging.com/en/glossary/apprenticeship)
- [**Asynchronous work**\\ \\ Asynchronous work is a style where teammates don't need to be available at the same time — decisions happen in writing, with response windows of hours or days instead of minutes. Required for genuinely distributed teams; useful for any team.\\ \\ Workplace & policy](https://join-staging.com/en/glossary/asynchronous-work)
- [**Automated rejection**\\ \\ Automated rejection is a rejection email sent without manual review — typically triggered by a knockout question, a stage move, or an AI-screening score below threshold. Faster than silence, riskier than written-by-human.\\ \\ AI & automation](https://join-staging.com/en/glossary/automated-rejection)

## B

- [**Background check**\\ \\ A background check is verification of facts a candidate stated about themselves — identity, work history, education, sometimes criminal record. Done with the candidate's consent.\\ \\ Process & stages](https://join-staging.com/en/glossary/background-check)
- [**Bar raiser**\\ \\ A bar raiser is a trained interviewer outside the hiring team with veto power on a hire. Popularized by Amazon. The role exists to keep the hiring bar from drifting as a team scales.\\ \\ Hiring methodology](https://join-staging.com/en/glossary/bar-raiser)
- [**Behavioral interview**\\ \\ A behavioral interview asks the candidate to describe specific past situations — what they did, with whom, what happened. The idea: past behavior predicts future behavior better than hypotheticals do.\\ \\ Process & stages](https://join-staging.com/en/glossary/behavioral-interview)
- [**Blind hiring**\\ \\ Blind hiring removes identifying information — name, photo, school, age — from applications during early review. The goal is to reduce unconscious bias in screening before interviews.\\ \\ Hiring methodology](https://join-staging.com/en/glossary/blind-hiring)
- [**Blue-collar worker**\\ \\ A blue-collar worker does manual or skilled-trade work — production, logistics, construction, care, hospitality. A huge share of hiring, and the part most ATS workflows are built badly for.\\ \\ Roles & people](https://join-staging.com/en/glossary/blue-collar-worker)
- [**Boolean search**\\ \\ Boolean search uses operators (AND, OR, NOT, quotes, parentheses) to narrow LinkedIn or Google searches for candidates. The core sourcing skill — and the only one that consistently produces matches no one else found.\\ \\ Sourcing & channels](https://join-staging.com/en/glossary/boolean-search)
- [**Boomerang employee**\\ \\ A boomerang employee is a former employee you rehire after they left. Known quantity, faster ramp, lower risk — if you understand why they left and what changed.\\ \\ Roles & people](https://join-staging.com/en/glossary/boomerang-employee)

## C

- [**C-level**\\ \\ C-level is the executive tier — the 'Chief …' roles (CEO, CFO, CTO, CPO) that own a whole function and answer to the board. Hiring them is executive search: equity-heavy comp, long timelines, board involvement.\\ \\ Roles & people](https://join-staging.com/en/glossary/c-level)
- [**Candidate experience**\\ \\ Candidate experience is how a candidate feels about your company from the moment they read your job posting to the moment they hear yes or no.\\ \\ Process & stages](https://join-staging.com/en/glossary/candidate-experience)
- [**Candidate persona**\\ \\ A candidate persona is a structured description of the type of candidate you're trying to attract for a role — background, motivations, channels, objections. Borrows the concept from marketing personas; applies it to sourcing and posting.\\ \\ Sourcing & channels](https://join-staging.com/en/glossary/candidate-persona)
- [**Career page**\\ \\ A career page is the public web page on a company's site that lists open roles and lets candidates apply. The first impression most candidates have of an employer.\\ \\ ATS & tools](https://join-staging.com/en/glossary/career-page)
- [**Chief people officer**\\ \\ The chief people officer (CPO) is the executive owner of HR, talent, and culture across the company. The C-suite seat for people — typically appearing in companies past 200-300 employees.\\ \\ Roles & people](https://join-staging.com/en/glossary/chief-people-officer)
- [**Coffee chat**\\ \\ A coffee chat is an informal, low-stakes 30-minute conversation early in the recruiting process — used to introduce a candidate to the team, surface mutual fit, and decide whether to start the formal process.\\ \\ Process & stages](https://join-staging.com/en/glossary/coffee-chat)
- [**Collaborative hiring**\\ \\ Collaborative hiring brings the whole team into the hiring process — peer interviews, shared decisions, distributed assessment — rather than running it as a recruiter-and-manager-only function.\\ \\ Hiring methodology](https://join-staging.com/en/glossary/collaborative-hiring)
- [**Conversational AI in recruiting**\\ \\ Conversational AI in recruiting is chatbots and assistants that handle candidate-facing conversations — answering FAQs, collecting application data, scheduling interviews, sending status updates. Always live, in the candidate's language.\\ \\ AI & automation](https://join-staging.com/en/glossary/conversational-ai-in-recruiting)
- [**Cost of vacancy**\\ \\ Cost of vacancy is what an unfilled role costs the business every day it stays open — lost output, overtime, and missed revenue. It's the number that makes slow hiring expensive.\\ \\ Metrics](https://join-staging.com/en/glossary/cost-of-vacancy)
- [**Cost per hire**\\ \\ Cost per hire is total recruiting spend over a period divided by the number of hires made. The number that turns hiring into a budget conversation.\\ \\ Metrics](https://join-staging.com/en/glossary/cost-per-hire)
- [**Counter offer**\\ \\ A counter offer is a new offer made by a candidate's current employer when the candidate announces they're leaving — typically a salary increase, sometimes a promotion or expanded scope. Common; usually a bad deal for the candidate.\\ \\ Compensation & benefits](https://join-staging.com/en/glossary/counter-offer)
- [**Cover letter**\\ \\ A cover letter is a short written piece a candidate submits alongside their CV — usually one page, explaining why they want this specific role at this specific company.\\ \\ Process & stages](https://join-staging.com/en/glossary/cover-letter)
- [**Cultural add**\\ \\ Cultural add evaluates whether a candidate will expand the team's range — perspective, skills, background — rather than match the existing average. The deliberate replacement for 'cultural fit'.\\ \\ Hiring methodology](https://join-staging.com/en/glossary/cultural-add)
- [**Cultural fit**\\ \\ Cultural fit is the classic criterion of whether a candidate matches a company's existing culture. Useful when it means shared values; a bias engine when it quietly means sameness — which is why many teams replaced it.\\ \\ Hiring methodology](https://join-staging.com/en/glossary/cultural-fit)
- [**CV**\\ \\ A CV (curriculum vitae) is the standard document a candidate uses to summarize their work history in Europe. The European equivalent of the American resume — different format, similar purpose.\\ \\ Process & stages](https://join-staging.com/en/glossary/cv)
- [**CV parsing**\\ \\ CV parsing is software extracting structured data — name, contact, skills, work history — from a CV or résumé and mapping it into an ATS's fields automatically, so recruiters don't retype it.\\ \\ AI & automation](https://join-staging.com/en/glossary/cv-parsing)

## D

- [**Digital nomad**\\ \\ A digital nomad is someone who works remotely while moving between locations or countries. Great for access to talent — if your payroll, tax, and time-zone setup can actually support it.\\ \\ Workplace & policy](https://join-staging.com/en/glossary/digital-nomad)
- [**Diversity hiring**\\ \\ Diversity hiring is the practice of widening the candidate pipeline to include groups underrepresented in your current team — without lowering the hiring bar. The pipeline widens; the bar holds.\\ \\ Hiring methodology](https://join-staging.com/en/glossary/diversity-hiring)

## E

- [**Employee referral**\\ \\ An employee referral is a candidate introduced by a current employee. Consistently the highest-quality and lowest-cost source of hire in most companies, and most under-invested-in at SMB scale.\\ \\ Sourcing & channels](https://join-staging.com/en/glossary/employee-referral)
- [**Employee resource group**\\ \\ An employee resource group (ERG) is a voluntary, employee-led group built around a shared identity or experience — women in tech, parents, LGBTQ+ — to support members and shape company policy.\\ \\ Workplace & policy](https://join-staging.com/en/glossary/employee-resource-group)
- [**Employee value proposition**\\ \\ An employee value proposition (EVP) is the deal you offer people for working at your company — pay, growth, culture, flexibility — stated as a promise you can actually keep.\\ \\ Sourcing & channels](https://join-staging.com/en/glossary/employee-value-proposition)
- [**Employer branding**\\ \\ Employer branding is the reputation your company has as a place to work — built from what employees say and what candidates experience in your hiring process.\\ \\ Sourcing & channels](https://join-staging.com/en/glossary/employer-branding)
- [**Employment contract**\\ \\ An employment contract is the legal agreement between employer and employee — role, compensation, working time, notice, governing law. Required in writing across most of Europe.\\ \\ Process & stages](https://join-staging.com/en/glossary/employment-contract)
- [**Entry-level**\\ \\ Entry-level roles target people with 0–2 years of experience — graduates, junior hires, career-changers. The defining challenge: screening for potential when there's no track record to read.\\ \\ Roles & people](https://join-staging.com/en/glossary/entry-level)
- [**Equal opportunity**\\ \\ Equal opportunity in hiring is the principle that every candidate is evaluated on their ability to do the role, not on protected characteristics. A legal requirement across the EU; a stated commitment in most companies.\\ \\ Compliance & legal](https://join-staging.com/en/glossary/equal-opportunity)
- [**Equity grant**\\ \\ An equity grant is an offer of ownership in the company — usually stock options or VSOPs — as part of a compensation package. Vests over time, typically 4 years with a 1-year cliff. Common in EU startups; rare in non-tech SMBs.\\ \\ Compensation & benefits](https://join-staging.com/en/glossary/equity-grant)
- [**EU AI Act in HR**\\ \\ The EU AI Act classifies AI used in employment decisions (screening, ranking, monitoring) as high-risk. Risk-management, transparency, human oversight, and bias-audit requirements apply to vendors and to the employers using them.\\ \\ AI & automation](https://join-staging.com/en/glossary/eu-ai-act-in-hr)

## F

- [**First-year retention**\\ \\ First-year retention is the percentage of hires still at the company 12 months after their start date. The cleanest lagging signal of whether your hiring and onboarding are working.\\ \\ Metrics](https://join-staging.com/en/glossary/first-year-retention)
- [**Flexitime**\\ \\ Flexitime is an arrangement where employees choose when within a day or week they do their working hours — within agreed core hours and total weekly hours. Standard in Germany, common across the EU.\\ \\ Workplace & policy](https://join-staging.com/en/glossary/flexitime)
- [**Four-day week**\\ \\ A four-day week is a working pattern where full-time employees work four days instead of five, usually for the same pay. Trialed across hundreds of European companies since 2022; results mixed but the candidate-attraction effect is consistent.\\ \\ Workplace & policy](https://join-staging.com/en/glossary/four-day-week)
- [**Freelancer**\\ \\ A freelancer is a self-employed person who provides services to clients under a contract rather than an employment relationship — paid per project or per hour, with no benefits and no payroll.\\ \\ Roles & people](https://join-staging.com/en/glossary/freelancer)
- [**Funnel conversion rate**\\ \\ Funnel conversion rate is the end-to-end percentage of applications that become hires for a role — typically 1-3% for European SMBs. The single number that summarizes overall funnel efficiency.\\ \\ Metrics](https://join-staging.com/en/glossary/funnel-conversion-rate)

## G

- [**GDPR in recruiting**\\ \\ GDPR governs how companies collect, store, and use personal data — including everything candidates submit during a hiring process. Compliance is mandatory in the EU and the bar is consent + minimization.\\ \\ Compliance & legal](https://join-staging.com/en/glossary/gdpr-recruiting)
- [**Generative AI in recruiting**\\ \\ Generative AI in recruiting is the use of large language models (ChatGPT-style) to produce text — job postings, outreach messages, rejection emails, interview summaries. Useful for drafting; risky as the final word.\\ \\ AI & automation](https://join-staging.com/en/glossary/generative-ai-in-recruiting)

## H

- [**Hard skills**\\ \\ Hard skills are teachable, measurable abilities — coding, accounting, welding, a second language. The part of a candidate you can actually test, as opposed to claim and believe.\\ \\ Hiring methodology](https://join-staging.com/en/glossary/hard-skills)
- [**Head of talent**\\ \\ The head of talent leads the recruiting function across the company — strategy, team, tools, hiring-bar discipline. The first dedicated TA leadership hire above the recruiter level.\\ \\ Roles & people](https://join-staging.com/en/glossary/head-of-talent)
- [**Hiring committee**\\ \\ A hiring committee is a small group with shared authority to decide whether a candidate moves forward or gets the offer. The opposite of one manager deciding alone.\\ \\ Process & stages](https://join-staging.com/en/glossary/hiring-committee)
- [**Hiring debrief**\\ \\ A hiring debrief is the structured discussion the interview team has after a final-round candidate, where written scorecards are compared and a decision is made. Usually 30 minutes.\\ \\ Process & stages](https://join-staging.com/en/glossary/hiring-debrief)
- [**Hiring funnel**\\ \\ The hiring funnel is the path a candidate moves through from first contact to offer — application, screening, interviews, offer, accept.\\ \\ Process & stages](https://join-staging.com/en/glossary/hiring-funnel)
- [**Hiring loop**\\ \\ A hiring loop is the full sequence of interviews a final-round candidate goes through in a single day or short window — often 3-5 sessions back-to-back. Compresses scheduling, intensifies the experience for both sides.\\ \\ Process & stages](https://join-staging.com/en/glossary/hiring-loop)
- [**Hiring manager**\\ \\ The hiring manager is the person the new hire will report to. They own the role, the criteria, and the final decision. The recruiter runs the process; the hiring manager owns the outcome.\\ \\ Roles & people](https://join-staging.com/en/glossary/hiring-manager)
- [**Hiring plan**\\ \\ A hiring plan is a one-page document agreed before a role opens — the criteria, the panel, the stages, the start-date target. The reference everyone uses when the process drifts.\\ \\ Process & stages](https://join-staging.com/en/glossary/hiring-plan)
- [**HR generalist**\\ \\ An HR generalist owns the whole HR function in a small company — recruiting, onboarding, policy, employee relations, payroll handoff. The Swiss-army-knife role most SMBs hire first.\\ \\ Roles & people](https://join-staging.com/en/glossary/hr-generalist)
- [**HRIS**\\ \\ HRIS (Human Resource Information System) is the software that stores the records of current employees — contracts, time off, payroll integrations, org chart. Different from an ATS, which handles candidates.\\ \\ ATS & tools](https://join-staging.com/en/glossary/hris)
- [**Hybrid work**\\ \\ Hybrid work is an arrangement where employees split time between the office and another location — usually with a minimum number of office days per week or per month. The dominant European model post-2022.\\ \\ Workplace & policy](https://join-staging.com/en/glossary/hybrid-work)

## I

- [**In-house recruiter**\\ \\ An in-house recruiter works directly for the company doing the hiring — employee, not contractor. The opposite of an external agency recruiter, who works for many companies.\\ \\ Roles & people](https://join-staging.com/en/glossary/in-house-recruiter)
- [**Internal mobility**\\ \\ Internal mobility is the practice of filling open roles with existing employees — promotions, lateral moves, role changes — before going to the external market.\\ \\ Hiring methodology](https://join-staging.com/en/glossary/internal-mobility)
- [**Internship program**\\ \\ An internship program is a structured set of paid internships offered each year — typically 3-6 months for students or recent graduates. Most reliable source of mid-funnel junior hires when run well; corrosive when run as cheap labor.\\ \\ Sourcing & channels](https://join-staging.com/en/glossary/internship-program)
- [**Interview calibration**\\ \\ Interview calibration is the practice of getting all interviewers on the same page about what 'good' looks like for a role — before any candidates are seen. The opposite of taste-based hiring.\\ \\ Process & stages](https://join-staging.com/en/glossary/interview-calibration)
- [**Interview scheduling**\\ \\ Interview scheduling is the work of putting a candidate, a hiring manager, and interviewers in the same calendar slot. The biggest hidden cost in SMB hiring.\\ \\ Process & stages](https://join-staging.com/en/glossary/interview-scheduling)
- [**Interview scorecard**\\ \\ An interview scorecard is the written form an interviewer fills out after each conversation — scores on agreed dimensions, written rationale, recommendation. The artifact that turns interviews into evidence.\\ \\ Process & stages](https://join-staging.com/en/glossary/interview-scorecard)

## J

- [**Job board**\\ \\ A job board is a website that aggregates and publishes job postings — Indeed, LinkedIn, StepStone, HelloWork, Infojobs. The default top-of-funnel for active candidates.\\ \\ Sourcing & channels](https://join-staging.com/en/glossary/job-board)
- [**Job description**\\ \\ A job description is the internal document defining a role's responsibilities, scope, and level — the source of truth for compensation calibration and performance review. Different from a job posting.\\ \\ Process & stages](https://join-staging.com/en/glossary/job-description)
- [**Job posting**\\ \\ A job posting is the public advert for an open role. What a candidate reads before deciding to apply — often the only view of your company before the click.\\ \\ Process & stages](https://join-staging.com/en/glossary/job-posting)
- [**Job shadowing**\\ \\ Job shadowing is observing someone do their job to learn how it actually works. A low-cost tool for onboarding, cross-training, and helping people decide if a role is right for them.\\ \\ Workplace & policy](https://join-staging.com/en/glossary/job-shadowing)

## K

- [**Knockout question**\\ \\ A knockout question is a yes/no question on the application form that auto-rejects candidates who don't meet a non-negotiable requirement — right-to-work, mandatory certification, language minimum.\\ \\ ATS & tools](https://join-staging.com/en/glossary/knockout-question)

## M

- [**Mid-level**\\ \\ Mid-level describes the individual contributor who can own a problem end to end without supervision — roughly 3–6 years in. It's the most-hired band and the hardest to interview well.\\ \\ Roles & people](https://join-staging.com/en/glossary/mid-level)
- [**Multiposting**\\ \\ Multiposting is publishing one job posting to several job boards at once from a single source — instead of pasting the listing into each board by hand.\\ \\ Sourcing & channels](https://join-staging.com/en/glossary/multiposting)

## N

- [**Notice period**\\ \\ A notice period is the time between an employee announcing they're leaving and their last working day — or between an employer firing them and the same. Set by contract and statute.\\ \\ Process & stages](https://join-staging.com/en/glossary/notice-period)

## O

- [**Offboarding**\\ \\ Offboarding is the structured exit process when someone leaves a company — handover, access removal, exit interview, final pay. The mirror image of onboarding.\\ \\ Process & stages](https://join-staging.com/en/glossary/offboarding)
- [**Offer acceptance rate**\\ \\ Offer acceptance rate is the percentage of candidates who accept an offer when one is extended. The clearest single signal of how competitive your offers and process are.\\ \\ Metrics](https://join-staging.com/en/glossary/offer-acceptance-rate)
- [**Offer letter**\\ \\ An offer letter is the written document an employer sends to formalize an offer to a candidate — title, compensation, start date, key conditions. The bridge between verbal yes and signed contract.\\ \\ Process & stages](https://join-staging.com/en/glossary/offer-letter)
- [**Onboarding**\\ \\ Onboarding is the structured first weeks of a new hire's time at a company — paperwork, tools, introductions, and the first work they actually do. It begins when the offer is signed.\\ \\ Process & stages](https://join-staging.com/en/glossary/onboarding)
- [**OTE (on-target earnings)**\\ \\ OTE — on-target earnings — is the total cash compensation a salesperson earns when they hit 100% of their quota. Base salary plus variable at target. The standard compensation framing for sales roles.\\ \\ Compensation & benefits](https://join-staging.com/en/glossary/ote)

## P

- [**Panel interview**\\ \\ A panel interview has 2-4 interviewers in the room (or call) at the same time, taking turns. The single conversation lets the team see how the candidate handles multiple stakeholders.\\ \\ Process & stages](https://join-staging.com/en/glossary/panel-interview)
- [**Parental leave**\\ \\ Parental leave is the statutory and contractual right of employees to take time off when a child is born or adopted, with job protection. Varies enormously by country in the EU; a strong candidate-attraction signal when offered above the legal minimum.\\ \\ Compensation & benefits](https://join-staging.com/en/glossary/parental-leave)
- [**Passive candidate**\\ \\ A passive candidate is someone not currently job-hunting, but who would consider an interesting move if approached. Industry estimates suggest 60-70% of the EU workforce sits in this category at any time.\\ \\ Sourcing & channels](https://join-staging.com/en/glossary/passive-candidate)
- [**Pay equity**\\ \\ Pay equity is the principle that employees doing equal work — or work of equal value — should be paid equally, regardless of gender, ethnicity, or other protected characteristics. Legal floor in the EU; a measurable internal discipline for SMBs.\\ \\ Compensation & benefits](https://join-staging.com/en/glossary/pay-equity)
- [**Pay transparency**\\ \\ Pay transparency is the practice of making compensation information visible — to candidates (salary in job postings), to employees (band visibility), or to regulators (pay-gap reporting). EU directives are making this mandatory in stages.\\ \\ Compensation & benefits](https://join-staging.com/en/glossary/pay-transparency)
- [**People analytics**\\ \\ People analytics applies data analysis to workforce questions — who's leaving, what hires perform best, where the funnel breaks. The discipline behind serious HR decisions; AI is one tool, not the whole thing.\\ \\ AI & automation](https://join-staging.com/en/glossary/people-analytics)
- [**People operations**\\ \\ People operations is the modern reframing of HR — focused on systems, data, and employee experience rather than compliance and paperwork alone. Common in tech-adjacent SMBs.\\ \\ Roles & people](https://join-staging.com/en/glossary/people-operations)
- [**Performance bonus**\\ \\ A performance bonus is variable compensation paid when an employee or the company hits agreed targets — typically annual, sometimes quarterly. Common for individual contributors in some industries, expected for managers and executives in most.\\ \\ Compensation & benefits](https://join-staging.com/en/glossary/performance-bonus)
- [**Phone screen**\\ \\ A phone screen is a short structured call — 20-30 minutes — between recruiter and candidate, early in the funnel. Confirms basics, checks motivation, and decides whether to advance to the hiring manager.\\ \\ Process & stages](https://join-staging.com/en/glossary/phone-screen)
- [**Pre-employment assessment**\\ \\ A pre-employment assessment is a structured test administered before hire — cognitive ability, skills, personality, or work-style measures. Used to add a standardized signal to the interview-based evaluation. Common in higher-volume hiring.\\ \\ Process & stages](https://join-staging.com/en/glossary/pre-employment-assessment)
- [**Preboarding**\\ \\ Preboarding is the period between offer acceptance and day one — when the new hire is committed but not yet working. The window where ghosting risk is highest.\\ \\ Process & stages](https://join-staging.com/en/glossary/preboarding)
- [**Probation period**\\ \\ A probation period is the initial months of an employment contract during which either side can end the relationship with shortened notice. Typically 3-6 months in Europe.\\ \\ Process & stages](https://join-staging.com/en/glossary/probation-period)
- [**Proximity bias**\\ \\ Proximity bias is the tendency to favour people who are physically present — more visibility, more credit, more promotions — over equally good remote colleagues. The quiet tax that breaks hybrid teams.\\ \\ Workplace & policy](https://join-staging.com/en/glossary/proximity-bias)

## Q

- [**Quality of hire**\\ \\ Quality of hire is a measure of how good a hire turned out to be — usually a composite of first-year performance rating, retention, and manager satisfaction. Hard to measure but the only metric that ultimately matters.\\ \\ Metrics](https://join-staging.com/en/glossary/quality-of-hire)

## R

- [**Recruiter**\\ \\ A recruiter is the person responsible for running the hiring process end-to-end — sourcing candidates, screening, coordinating interviews, managing the offer. Sometimes a dedicated role, often a shared one in an SMB.\\ \\ Roles & people](https://join-staging.com/en/glossary/recruiter)
- [**Recruitment agency**\\ \\ A recruitment agency is an external company that finds and presents candidates for a fee — usually 18-25% of first-year salary. The third-party alternative to building in-house recruiting capacity.\\ \\ Roles & people](https://join-staging.com/en/glossary/recruitment-agency)
- [**Recruitment coordinator**\\ \\ A recruitment coordinator handles the scheduling, candidate communications, and process logistics so the recruiter and hiring manager can focus on assessment. The role behind a fast process.\\ \\ Roles & people](https://join-staging.com/en/glossary/recruitment-coordinator)
- [**Recruitment CRM**\\ \\ A recruitment CRM (candidate relationship management) is the system for staying in touch with people who aren't applicants yet — sourced contacts, past finalists, alumni. The pipeline before the pipeline.\\ \\ ATS & tools](https://join-staging.com/en/glossary/recruitment-crm)
- [**Recruitment marketing**\\ \\ Recruitment marketing is the practice of attracting candidates before they're actively job-hunting — through employer brand content, talent community email, career-page SEO, conference presence. Builds top-of-funnel beyond the open-role posting.\\ \\ Sourcing & channels](https://join-staging.com/en/glossary/recruitment-marketing)
- [**Reference check**\\ \\ A reference check is a short conversation with a candidate's former manager or colleague to verify what the candidate told you and surface what they didn't. Done at the end of the process.\\ \\ Process & stages](https://join-staging.com/en/glossary/reference-check)
