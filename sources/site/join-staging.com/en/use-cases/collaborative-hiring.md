## Structured scorecards turn opinions into comparable decisions.

Define what 'good' looks like for a role, and every interviewer scores against the same criteria. Join collects each scorecard, shows them side by side, and makes it clear where the panel agrees and where it doesn't. The decision rests on evidence, not on whoever speaks loudest in the debrief.

- Shared criteria so feedback is comparable
- Side-by-side scores across the interview panel
- Fairer, faster, more defensible hiring calls

## Notes and @mentions keep the conversation on the candidate.

Interview impressions, reference notes, and next steps live on the candidate's profile, not in private chats and side emails. @mention a colleague to pull them in, and the full context travels with the candidate through every stage. Anyone who joins the loop late catches up in seconds.

- Team notes attached to each candidate profile
- @mention colleagues to bring them into the loop
- Full context, so no one re-asks the same questions

Does Join charge per user for collaboration?

No. Join includes unlimited seats, so you can add every hiring manager, interviewer, and coordinator without per-seat fees. Collaboration is the default, not a paid upgrade.

How do interview scorecards work?

You define the evaluation criteria for a role, and each interviewer scores the candidate against those same criteria after their interview. Join collects the scorecards and displays them side by side, surfacing consensus and disagreement so the panel decides on evidence rather than recall.

Can hiring managers use Join without recruiter training?

Yes. Managers get a focused view of their candidates, leave structured feedback, and @mention colleagues, with no recruiting expertise needed. Roles and permissions keep their experience simple and their access scoped.

How does Join keep candidate feedback organized?

All notes, scorecards, and messages attach to the candidate's profile and travel with them through every stage. Anyone added to the loop sees the complete history instantly, so feedback never gets lost in private chats or email.

Can we control who sees what?

Yes. Roles and permissions let you give hiring managers access to their own roles, keep salary or sensitive notes restricted, and maintain a clean audit trail. It's collaboration with the right guardrails.
