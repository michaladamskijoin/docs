## Knockout questions filter on apply. No CV stack to work through.

The slowest step in most pipelines is a recruiter working through a stack of CVs. Join's Knockout questions filter out applicants who don't meet your must-haves automatically. Your best applicants reach a human without waiting behind a pile of mismatches.

- Knockout questions filter must-have mismatches automatically
- Every decision logged with the answer and the rule that matched
- No CV stacks, no week-long screening lag

## Self-service scheduling kills the back-and-forth that eats days.

Coordinating interviews over email can add a week per candidate. Share your availability and let candidates book a slot that works for them. It syncs to your calendar and sends reminders that cut no-shows. The handoff from screen to interview takes hours, not days of 'does Tuesday work?'

- Candidates self-book from your live availability
- Calendar sync and automatic reminders
- Hours from screen to interview, not days

How does Join reduce time-to-fill?

Join compresses the three slowest stages of hiring: posting (one click to 250+ boards the same day), screening (Knockout questions remove must-have mismatches automatically, no manual CV backlog), and scheduling (candidate self-booking with calendar sync). Removing the delays between these steps is what pulls time-to-fill down from months toward days.

Does faster hiring mean lower-quality hires?

No. The speed comes from removing waiting, not from skipping rigor. Structured screening surfaces qualified candidates sooner, and scorecards keep evaluation consistent. You move fast on the right people instead of slowly on everyone.

What slows hiring down the most, and does Join fix it?

The biggest delays are usually slow posting, CV-screening backlogs, and interview scheduling ping-pong. Join addresses all three directly with multiposting, knockout screening, and self-service scheduling, the three stages where days quietly disappear.

Can I see where my pipeline is slow?

Yes. Join's analytics show how long candidates spend in each stage and where time-to-fill leaks, so you can target the actual bottleneck rather than guessing.

Do candidates really get more applications with Quick Apply?

On the seven boards that support it, candidates apply in one click straight from the board with no redirect. Per board, customers see up to 3x more applications than standard click-through ads. That's more candidates, arriving faster.
