## How to read the ratio

Typical European SMB ranges:

- **5-20 applicants per hire**: tight pipeline. Either a hard-to-fill role or you’re undersourcing.
- **30-100 per hire**: healthy range for most roles.
- **300+ per hire**: high-volume inbound. Strong [employer brand](https://join-staging.com/en/glossary/employer-branding) or a low-bar role; either way, [screening](https://join-staging.com/en/glossary/screening) process is the bottleneck.

The interesting reading isn’t the raw number — it’s the trend over time for the same role type, and the gap between similar roles.

## What the number actually tells you

Three things:

- **Top-of-funnel demand**: low ratios on roles where you expect high demand suggest a job-posting or distribution problem.
- **Selectivity**: high ratios on roles where you’re aiming wide suggest your bar is well-calibrated.
- **Time investment**: every [application](https://join-staging.com/en/glossary/application-form) reviewed costs 1-3 minutes of [recruiter](https://join-staging.com/en/glossary/recruiter) time. 500 applicants per hire × 2 minutes = 17 hours of [screening](https://join-staging.com/en/glossary/screening) per hire, just on rejected applications.

## What it doesn’t tell you

Quality. A role can have 200 applicants per hire and still hire a mediocre candidate if the screening criteria are wrong. Pair this with [quality of hire](https://join-staging.com/en/glossary/quality-of-hire) to avoid optimizing for the wrong number.

## When the ratio is the metric to optimize

If you’re hitting time-to-hire targets and [quality of hire](https://join-staging.com/en/glossary/quality-of-hire) is strong, applicants per hire is the cost lever. Lower it via better job-posting writing (filter early), better knockout questions, better source-channel mix.
