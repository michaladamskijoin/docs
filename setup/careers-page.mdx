Join gives you two ways to publish your open roles to candidates: a hosted careers page on a Join subdomain, or a jobs widget you embed on your existing website. Both paths run on the same underlying pipeline — the same application form, the same SEO defaults, and the same candidate data flowing into your Join workspace.

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Hosted careers page">
    The hosted option gets you a live, branded careers page in minutes with no DNS changes and no developer involvement.

    <Steps>
      <Step title="Go to Settings → Careers page">
        Open **Settings** and click **Careers page** in the left-hand menu.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Upload your logo and set brand colors">
        Upload your company logo and choose your primary brand color. Join applies both to the page header and the apply buttons so the page matches your brand.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Add a company description and social links (optional)">
        Optionally add a short company description and links to your LinkedIn, Instagram, or other social profiles. These appear on the careers page to give candidates context about your company before they apply.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Click Publish">
        Click **Publish**. Your careers page goes live immediately at `yourcompany.join.com/careers`.
      </Step>
    </Steps>

    <Tip>
      No DNS changes or developer work needed. Free SSL is provisioned automatically.
    </Tip>
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Embed on your website">
    The jobs widget lets your open roles live at your own domain, preserving your SEO equity while Join handles all the underlying data.

    <Steps>
      <Step title="Go to Settings → Careers page → Jobs widget">
        Open **Settings**, click **Careers page**, then select the **Jobs widget** tab.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Copy the embed code snippet">
        Click **Copy code** to copy the widget snippet to your clipboard.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Paste the code into your site">
        Paste the snippet into your WordPress, Webflow, or Wix page — or directly into any HTML `<body>` tag on a custom site. No additional configuration is required.

        ```html
        <script
          src="https://widget.join.com/v1/embed.js"
          data-company-id="YOUR_COMPANY_ID">
        </script>
        <div id="join-jobs"></div>
        ```
      </Step>
      <Step title="Jobs update automatically">
        Every time you publish or archive a job in your Join workspace, the widget on your site updates live — no redeployment or manual edits needed.
      </Step>
    </Steps>
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## What candidates experience

Regardless of which hosting option you choose, every candidate goes through the same streamlined application flow.

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Mobile-first application form" icon="mobile-screen">
    The application form is designed for mobile from the ground up. Candidates apply from any device without pinching, zooming, or fighting a desktop layout.
  </Card>
  <Card title="CV upload — no account required" icon="file-arrow-up">
    Candidates upload their CV and submit. They don't need to create a Join account. Join parses the CV automatically and populates the candidate record in your pipeline.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Automatic language detection" icon="language">
    The careers page and application form render in the candidate's browser language automatically. No language picker is needed and no manual translation work is required from you.
  </Card>
  <Card title="One unified pipeline" icon="layer-group">
    Applications from your careers page, your embedded widget, and every job board Join posts to all land in the same view inside Join. You never need to check multiple inboxes.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## SEO defaults

Both hosting options apply the same SEO configuration to every published role automatically — no settings to toggle.

| Feature | Detail |
|---|---|
| JobPosting schema | Structured data markup on every role page, readable by Google and other search engines |
| Auto-submitted sitemap | Join submits your sitemap whenever you publish or update a job |
| Google for Jobs | Roles are eligible to appear directly in Google Search results |
| Per-role meta tags | Each job gets its own title tag and meta description drawn from the job details |

<Info>
  Both hosting options use the same candidate pipeline — applications from your careers page and from job boards all appear in the same view in Join.
</Info>
